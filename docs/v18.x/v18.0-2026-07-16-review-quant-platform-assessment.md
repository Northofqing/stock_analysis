# v18.0 Quant Platform Assessment

**Date:** 2026-07-16
**Scope:** current repository evidence, public institutional practices, and the requirements in `AGENTS.md`.
**Conclusion:** the project has a credible event-driven A-share monitoring and paper-trading foundation, but it is not yet an auditable, research-to-live trading system. v18 should close the research-to-paper loop first and treat live execution as a separately earned capability.

## 1. Evidence and method

This review inspected the production monitor integration surface, data providers, data-quality/freshness checks, trading/risk modules, database schemas, backtesting/review code, versioned designs, and the library test suite.

`cargo test --lib` completed with **1,164 passed, 0 failed, 7 ignored**. It emitted warnings, including unused code, deprecated dispatcher usage, and an unreachable pattern. Therefore, the suite is evidence of broad unit coverage, not release readiness under `clippy -D warnings`.

The mandatory `.github/copilot-instructions.md` file named by `AGENTS.md` was absent. No contents were inferred.

## 3. Quantitative engineering and data assessment

### 3.1 Strengths to preserve

- The daily-bar fallback accepts a source only after `validate_daily_kline_quality`; all providers failing returns an error rather than fabricated bars. This aligns with 2.1 and 2.3.
- The paper leg explicitly writes `paper_trades` and is isolated from real `stock_position` data. This is the right direction for 2.5.
- Existing risk checks include A-share lot-size, price-limit, cash/position, account-mode and duplicate-ID concepts. These should become one shared pre-trade policy rather than be recreated.
- The code already recognizes factor snapshots, walk-forward concerns, data freshness, and post-trade review as first-class concerns.

### 3.2 Blocking weaknesses

| Priority | Finding | Risk | Red-line relationship | v18 response |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| P0 | `DataFetchService` converts failed financial, money-flow and intraday fetches to empty/default values | missing data can look like valid zero signal | 2.1, 2.2 | all decision inputs become `Available`, `Missing`, `Stale`, or `Invalid`; only `Available` may affect a trade decision |
| P0 | Some monitor paths synthesize fresh capabilities or assign `DataMode::Full` | health status can be a claim rather than a fact | 2.3, 2.4 | one timestamped `DataHealthSnapshot` is the sole authority; synthetic health is test-only |

| P1 | Research fallback may use slice-end factors when snapshots are unavailable | look-ahead bias and invalid performance claims | 2.1, 2.3 | snapshot completeness is mandatory for promotion; legacy results marked exploratory |

| P1 | No common provenance key joins source data, model version, decision, paper trade, review outcome, and future broker fill | cannot explain or falsify a result | 2.7 | immutable `decision_id` and correlation IDs across the full loop |

## 4. Product-closure assessment

The product currently delivers valuable monitoring and decision support, but its loop breaks between recommendation and verified economic outcome:

```text
market/news → signals → notification/recommendation → paper trade (partial)
                                                └── no unified decision provenance
paper trade → review (partial) → model/risk update (not governed)
real order → broker report → reconciliation → P&L attribution (not proven)
```

The v18 product definition is therefore not “more signals.” It is a trustworthy answer to these product questions:

1. What data, as of what time, produced this signal?
2. Which model/configuration and portfolio constraints approved or rejected it?
3. Under a stated fill/cost model, what would have happened?
4. Did the outcome validate, falsify, or place limits on the hypothesis?
5. If live trading is enabled later, does broker truth exactly reconcile to the internal ledger?

## 5. Institutional-practice reference points

The following are public references, not claims about proprietary systems or a direct prescription for A-share regulation.

- AQR treats transaction costs as an input to portfolio construction and assesses performance net of implementation cost. v18 therefore makes expected cost, liquidity, and fill uncertainty inputs to sizing, not a report appended after trading. [AQR: Transaction Costs](https://www.aqr.com/insights/research/white-papers/transactions-costs-practical-application)
- AQR frames portfolio construction, risk management, and cost control as implementation alpha. v18 separates these concerns from alpha generation. [AQR: The Alpha in Portfolio Construction](https://www.aqr.com/Insights/Research/Trade-Publication/The-Alpha-in-Portfolio-Construction)
- Man Group's public work highlights sequence-level market impact; v18 designs a parent-order/child-order and transaction-cost-analysis boundary even before enabling a broker. [Man Group: Counting the Costs](https://www.man.com/insights/counting-the-costs)
- Man Group publicly distinguishes alpha research, portfolio construction, risk management, and execution research; v18 mirrors those product seams. [Man Group role overview](https://www.man.com/graduate-programmes)
- SEC market-access guidance is used as a safety benchmark for pre-trade limits, authorized access, post-trade reporting, and documented review; it is not represented as a PRC legal requirement. [SEC Rule 15c3-5](https://www.sec.gov/rules-regulations/2011/06/risk-management-controls-brokers-or-dealers-market-access)
- Federal Reserve model-risk guidance motivates a model inventory, validation, monitoring, and effective challenge. [SR 26-2](https://www.federalreserve.gov/supervisionreg/srletters/SR2602.htm)

## 6. Recommendation

Build v18 in two product stages:

1. **Institutional paper loop:** reproducible data and research, net-cost simulation, portfolio/risk sizing, durable decision/paper/fill/outcome records, and objective review.
2. **Controlled live-execution gate:** only after the paper loop has stable evidence and the broker lifecycle, reconciliation, operational controls, and human approval requirements are proven.

This sequencing maximizes learning while respecting 2.1–2.7. It prevents a dangerous category error: treating a push, a manual adjustment, or a simulated fill as a live trade.
